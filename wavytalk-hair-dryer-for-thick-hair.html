Why Thick Hair Needs a Powerful Dryer

If you have thick hair, you already know the struggle. Standard 1200-1400W dryers take 20-30 minutes to get through dense strands. Your arms ache. The motor overheats. And by the time you’re done, the first section you dried is already frizzy from ambient moisture.

Thick hair — whether it’s coarse, dense, curly, or a combination — demands three things from a dryer:

  • High wattage (1800W+): More power means more airflow volume pushing through dense hair. Anything under 1800W simply can’t move enough air to penetrate multiple layers of thick strands efficiently.
  • Ionic technology: Thick hair has more surface area, which means more opportunity for static and frizz. Negative ions break down water molecules faster and neutralize the positive charge that causes flyaways.
  • The right attachments: A concentrator alone won’t cut it. Thick hair benefits enormously from comb attachments that simultaneously detangle, stretch, and direct airflow deep into sections — rather than just blowing across the surface.

The Wavytalk Blown Away checks all three boxes on paper: 1875W motor, ionic ceramic technology, and a dedicated comb attachment. But specs are just specs. What matters is whether real people with thick hair are getting results. Let’s look at what they report.

Potential Drawbacks for Thick Hair

No dryer is perfect, and transparency matters. Here are the concerns that thick-hair buyers should consider before purchasing:

⚠ What to Watch For

  • Motor longevity under heavy use: Some buyers report the motor getting loud after several months of heavy use. Thick hair requires longer drying sessions and more frequent use, which puts more strain on the motor than fine hair would. At $33 this is an acceptable trade-off for most, but if you’re drying thick hair daily, be aware the motor may not last as long as a $150+ professional dryer.
  • Button placement: The on/off, temperature, and cool shot controls are positioned where your fingers naturally grip. During extended thick-hair drying sessions (even 10 minutes requires continuous holding), you may accidentally press buttons and change settings. Most buyers adapt their grip within a few uses.
  • 125V US only: This dryer operates on 125V power only. If you travel internationally and need a dryer that handles thick hair abroad, this is not the right choice. Look for a dual-voltage (100-240V) model instead.
  • Not a salon replacement: If you’re a professional stylist drying thick hair on multiple clients per day, this dryer isn’t built for that workload. It’s designed for home use — 1-2 sessions per day maximum.
  • Cord length: Some buyers find the cord too short, which can be annoying when you need to reach around a thick head of hair from different angles. A bathroom outlet extension may be helpful.

The durability concern is the most relevant one for thick-hair users specifically. Thick hair means longer sessions, more heat exposure to the motor, and more frequent use overall. If the motor fails after 6-8 months of daily heavy use, you’re still paying less per year than a single premium dryer. But it’s worth setting realistic expectations.
