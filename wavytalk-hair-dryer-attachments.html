Comb Attachment: The Fan Favorite for Thick & Natural Hair

Best For

Thick hair, coarse hair, 4C hair, natural hair of all textures. If your hair is dense and takes forever to dry, this is your attachment.

What It Does

The comb attachment is a game-changer for anyone with thick or natural hair. Unlike a standard nozzle that only blows air across the surface, the comb physically separates strands while directing heated airflow between each tooth. This means you’re doing three things at once: detangling, stretching, and drying. For 4C coils that normally shrink back up during drying, the gentle tension from the comb keeps hair elongated without the direct-contact heat damage of a flat iron.

Think of it as a blow-dry brush built into your dryer — except lighter, simpler, and already included in the box. No juggling a brush in one hand and a dryer in the other. The comb does both jobs simultaneously.

Technique

  1. Section your hair into 4-6 parts with clips. Even with the comb’s penetrating airflow, working in sections ensures every strand gets fully dried.
  2. Start at the ends and work upward toward the roots. This prevents snagging and breakage, especially on natural hair that’s prone to tangles.
  3. Pull slowly: Don’t rush the comb through. Let the airflow do the work. A slow, steady pull gives the heat time to dry each section thoroughly as it passes.
  4. Use medium-high heat and high speed. The combination of strong airflow and moderate heat dries thick hair quickly without scorching. High speed pushes air deep into dense sections; medium-high heat avoids heat damage on repeated passes.
  5. Finish with cool shot: Once a section is dry and stretched, hit the cool shot button for 5-10 seconds to lock in the shape and add shine.

Key Result

Entire head dried in approximately 10 minutes for thick hair. Stretched, smooth, and detangled — often eliminating the need for a follow-up flat iron session. For 4C hair specifically, buyers report achieving elongation that previously required a dedicated blow dryer brush or tension method.

Who Loves It

The comb is the clear fan favorite in the natural hair community. Among verified buyers with 4C, thick, and coarse hair, the comb attachment is consistently the most-mentioned and most-praised accessory. Buyers who previously spent 20+ minutes drying thick hair with a standard nozzle describe cutting that time in half. One verified buyer summarized it: “I use the comb the most — gets the job done in 10 mins.”

Diffuser Attachment: Define Curls Without the Frizz

Best For

Curly hair (3A-3C) and wavy hair (2A-2C). If you want to enhance your natural texture without disturbing curl formation, the diffuser is your go-to.

What It Does

The diffuser disperses the dryer’s concentrated airstream into a wide, gentle flow. Instead of a focused blast that blows curls apart and creates frizz, the bowl shape and prongs cradle your curls while warm air circulates around them evenly. This sets curl definition faster than air-drying alone while preserving the natural pattern your styling products created.

Combined with the Wavytalk’s ionic technology, the diffuser does double duty: the dispersed airflow protects curl structure while negative ions smooth the cuticle and reduce frizz. For curly and wavy hair types, frizz is the enemy — and this combination addresses it from two angles simultaneously.

Technique

  1. Apply your styling products first (gel, mousse, or curl cream) to wet hair. The diffuser sets whatever pattern your products create, so start with well-defined curls.
  2. Scrunch curls into the bowl: Flip your head or tilt to the side. Gather a section of curls and gently place them into the diffuser bowl. Let the prongs cradle the curls without pulling or stretching them.
  3. Hold still for 20-30 seconds: This is critical. Do not move the diffuser once it’s positioned. Movement disrupts curl clumps and creates frizz. Let the warm air set the curl in place before moving to the next section.
  4. Use LOW heat and LOW speed: Curly hair is more fragile and prone to frizz from heat. Low settings give you control without overwhelming delicate curl patterns. Patience here pays off in definition.
  5. Don’t touch your hair: Resist the urge to scrunch or rake through curls while diffusing. Hands introduce frizz. Let the diffuser and gravity do the work.

Key Result

Defined, bouncy curls without the 2-3 hour air-dry wait. The diffuser cuts drying time significantly while maintaining curl integrity — no crunchy, frizzy, or flat results. For 2A-2C wavy hair, it enhances the wave pattern into more defined, consistent waves that would otherwise fall flat during a slow air-dry.

Who Loves It

Curly and wavy hair buyers praise the diffuser for preserving their natural texture. One verified buyer with curly hair stated: “I particularly love the diffuser, as it enhances my natural curls without causing any frizz.” The ionic technology is doing measurable work here — curly hair has more surface area exposed to friction and static, and the negative ions neutralize that effectively.

Concentrator Nozzle: Precision Blowouts & Smoothing

Best For

Straight hair, fine hair, and blowout styles. If your goal is a sleek, salon-smooth finish with volume and movement, the concentrator is your tool.

What It Does

The concentrator nozzle narrows the Wavytalk’s airflow into a focused, directional stream. This gives you precise control over where the air hits — essential for smoothing the cuticle layer flat, directing hair around a round brush, and creating polished blowout results. Without a concentrator, air blows in all directions and can rough up the cuticle, causing frizz and a puffy finish. With it, you control exactly where heat and air go.

For fine or straight hair that doesn’t need detangling or curl preservation, the concentrator is the most useful attachment. It transforms the dryer from a general-purpose tool into a precision styling instrument.

Technique

  1. Section your hair and start from the bottom layers. Work your way up so finished sections don’t get disturbed by wet hair above them.
  2. Point the nozzle downward along the hair shaft, always following the direction hair grows (roots to ends). This smooths the cuticle flat rather than roughing it up, which is the key to a frizz-free, shiny finish.
  3. Use with a round brush: Wrap a section around a round brush, then direct the concentrator nozzle at that section. The combination of tension from the brush and focused airflow creates volume at the root and smooth, turned-under ends.
  4. Keep the nozzle 2-3 inches from hair: The concentrated airflow gets hot at close range. Maintaining a small distance prevents heat damage while still directing airflow precisely.
  5. Finish with cool shot: After each section is dry and smooth, blast it with cool air to seal the cuticle and lock in the style. This adds shine and extends how long the blowout lasts.

Key Result

Salon-smooth blowout finish at home. Sleek, shiny hair with volume at the roots and smooth, polished ends. The concentrator nozzle paired with a round brush gives you the same technique professional stylists use — just with a $33 dryer instead of a $300 one.

Important Note

The concentrator nozzle gets hot during extended use. The focused airflow means heat concentrates at the tip of the nozzle. Do not touch the plastic tip during or immediately after use — let it cool before removing. This is normal for all concentrator nozzles and not a defect.

Who Loves It

Buyers who want a polished, straight blowout love the concentrator. It’s also the go-to attachment for stylists creating sleek updos, smoothing flyaways, or adding targeted volume at the crown. For fine hair that can look flat and limp after air-drying, the concentrator with a round brush adds body and movement that lasts all day.

Tips for Attaching & Removing

The Wavytalk attachments use a snap-on mechanism that locks them firmly to the barrel. This is by design — you don’t want an attachment flying off mid-dry. But some buyers note they can be a bit stubborn to remove, especially when new.

Here’s how to handle them:

Attaching

  • Align the attachment with the barrel opening. There’s a slight groove that indicates correct orientation.
  • Push firmly until you hear/feel a click. The attachment should sit flush against the dryer body with no wobble.
  • Give it a gentle tug to confirm it’s locked. If it moves, push harder until it fully clicks in.

Removing

  • Let the dryer cool first. Especially the concentrator nozzle, which retains heat at the tip.
  • Grip the attachment firmly (not the dryer body) and pull straight off with steady pressure. Don’t twist or rock side-to-side — this can stress the plastic.
  • If it feels stuck, run cool air for 10 seconds first. The slight contraction from cooling can make removal easier.
  • New attachments loosen up slightly after the first few uses as the plastic seats in. The initial tightness is normal.

One buyer in a mixed 3-star review noted: “The attachments snap on very securely. Compact.” The secure fit is intentional — it prevents the attachment from vibrating loose during use, which cheaper dryers often suffer from. The trade-off is slightly more effort to remove, which most buyers adapt to quickly.
