Best Technique for 4C Hair (Based on Buyer Tips)

Reviewers who got the best results on 4C/thick hair share these common steps:

1. Blow-Dry First

Multiple 4C reviewers confirm: blow-drying or pre-stretching hair before steaming gives dramatically better results. The tool isn't designed to go from wash-and-go to straight in one step.

2. Use Max Temperature (390–410°F)

4C hair needs the highest setting. Lower temps won't fully penetrate the coil pattern. The steam protects against damage even at higher heat.

3. Small Sections, Slow Passes

"One or two passes" is achievable, but only with 1-2 inch sections. Trying to do too much hair at once reduces effectiveness.

4. Refill Water 2-3 Times

For a full head of thick 4C hair, expect to refill the water reservoir 2-3 times. This adds maybe 2 minutes total — negligible vs the time saved.

Who Should Buy This for 4C Hair?

Buy it if:

  • You currently spend 1-3 hours flat-ironing and want to cut that to under 30 minutes
  • You value hair health over maximum hold time
  • You straighten 2-3x per week anyway (so shorter hold doesn't matter)

Skip it if:

  • You need results that last 5-7+ days between washes
  • You have fine/thin hair (flat iron is better for your type)
  • You want pin-straight ends with a curl/flip (flat iron is more precise)
  • You refuse to blow-dry first (the tool works best on pre-stretched hair)
